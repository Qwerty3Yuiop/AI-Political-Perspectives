Summary from the AllSides News Team

President Joe Biden announced Wednesday that the U.S. would purchase 500 million more doses of the Pfizer COVID-19 vaccine to donate to other countries, bringing America’s total donation commitment to over 1.1 billion doses. The effort is motivated by Biden’s reported goal of fully vaccinating 70% of the world’s population within the next year. While the U.S. has fully vaccinated 55% of its population, many poorer countries have vaccination rates below 2%. “To date, the U.S. has already shipped nearly 160 million doses to 100 countries – donating more vaccines than all other countries combined,” the White House said in a Wednesday fact sheet on Biden’s Global COVID-19 Summit. 

Coverage was mostly balanced across the spectrum but more common in left- and center-rated outlets on Wednesday.
