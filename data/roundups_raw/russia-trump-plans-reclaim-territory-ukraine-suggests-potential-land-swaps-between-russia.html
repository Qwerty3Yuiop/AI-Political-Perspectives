Summary from the AllSides News Team

President Trump has announced his intention to try and reclaim territory for Ukraine during his Friday meeting with Russian President Vladimir Putin.

The Details: Trump said he plans to push for the return of certain territories to Ukraine, saying “Russia's occupied a big portion of Ukraine. We're going to try to get some of that territory back.” Trump and Putin are due to hold talks in Alaska to discuss an end to the Russia-Ukraine war. Trump said Friday is a “feel out meeting,” and suggested that there could be "some swapping, changes in land" between Russia and Ukraine. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said any agreements without input from Kyiv would amount to "dead decisions.” 

For Context: Putin previously said Russia would agree to a complete ceasefire if Ukraine withdrew its troops from all of the Donetsk province, leaving Russia in full control of the Donbas region, but Zelenskyy has rejected this deal. Last month, Trump said he was "disappointed" by Putin after agreements to an immediate ceasefire fell apart.

How the Media Covered It: BBC News (Center bias) emphasized Trump’s effort to regain Ukrainian territory, mentioning Trump’s intent to speak to Zelenskyy right after the meeting "out of respect.” It included input from EU leaders who said any deal to end the war “must include Ukraine and Europe more widely.” Bloomberg (Lean Left) wrote Trump was “downplaying expectations” for the meeting, saying it “represents a high-stakes gamble” for him. It framed the meeting as leaving out Zelenskyy and other European leaders who are “fearful” Trump will “concede too much.” Fox News (Right) highlighted Trump’s criticism of Zelenskyy and his objection to “land swapping.” It reiterated Trump’s belief that Ukraine was in part at fault for Russia’s 2022 invasion, and Trump’s belief that if he had won the 2020 election, the war wouldn’t have happened.
