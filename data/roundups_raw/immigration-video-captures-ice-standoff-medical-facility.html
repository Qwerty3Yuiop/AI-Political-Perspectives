Summary from the AllSides News Team

Staff at the Ontario Advanced Surgery Center in California tried to block Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents from arresting a Honduran landscaper this week. A video clip of the altercation was posted on social media.

The Details: Federal immigration agents pursued 30-year-old Denis Guillen-Solis into a surgery center, where clinic staff demanded to see identification and a warrant. The situation escalated as staff physically blocked agents, locked the doors, obstructed vehicles, and reportedly called the police claiming a possible "kidnapping." The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) stated on X that the ICE officers were conducting a targeted operation to arrest two men who were in the country illegally. Solis was arrested following the standoff.

For Context: ICE has become increasingly public in detaining migrants, conducting operations in immigration courts and workplaces. This incident is the latest in a series of confrontations between federal immigration officers and the public. Solis was sending money to Honduras to help his mother with her dialysis treatments. Follow our updates on other controversial detainments and deportations.

How the Media Covered It: Fox News (Right bias) focused on quotes from DHS about the incident, emphasizing how the surgery center staff blocked ICE agents. It mentioned other recent incidents between ICE and immigration protestors. Associated Press (Left) quoted Javier Hernandez, executive director of the Inland Coalition for Immigrant Justice, who called the staffers “brave”. It also mentioned recent ICE arrests, but highlighted attempts by bystanders and a Catholic bishop to prevent them. Both outlets included the information about Solis' mother.
