Summary from the AllSides News Team

The FBI reportedly conducted two searches at the University of Delaware in recent weeks as part of its probe of President Joe Biden’s improper storing of classified documents.

The Details: According to multiple reports citing anonymous sources, investigators are not thought to have found any classified documents at the university, but recovered some documents that are currently under review. Biden approved of and cooperated with the search. The FBI reportedly looked through two sets of documents during the searches, including some from Biden's time in the Senate.

For Context: The searches are the latest step in the federal investigation of the president's mishandling of classified documents, which the Department of Justice (DOJ) appointed a special counsel to probe. A trove of classified materials was discovered at Biden's Delaware home, and at his think tank in Washington, D.C. Former President Donald Trump is also being investigated for improperly storing classified documents at his Mar-a-Lago estate, and his vice president Mike Pence was also found to have classified documents at his Indiana home.

How the Media Covered It: Sources across the spectrum covered CNN's report, with some right-rated outlets highlighting it more prominently than left- and center-rated sources did.
