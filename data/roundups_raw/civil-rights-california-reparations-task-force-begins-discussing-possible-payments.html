Summary from the AllSides News Team

The California Reparations Task Force has begun discussion on possible payments to black Americans due to the "lingering effects" of slavery. 

Key Quotes: "It's a debt that's owed, we worked for free ... we're not asking; we're telling you," Max Fennell, a 35-year-old businessman and former professional triathlete, told the panel. "The tangibles of what I'm asking for is $350,000 per black American in California — that's tangible, small business grant $250,000, and land 15-20 acres."

For Context: In 2020, California Governor Gavin Newsom signed a bill establishing the reparations task force, which has been studying the issue and has begun public deliberations over how to quantify reparations to black Americans. California is one of a dozen states that now aim to address the issue. The task force's report and recommendations to the California state legislature are due July 1.

How the Media Covered It: The discussions of the California Reparations Task Force have been covered by sources across the political spectrum, with the left pointing out that other states like Texas are developing and studying reparation plans.
