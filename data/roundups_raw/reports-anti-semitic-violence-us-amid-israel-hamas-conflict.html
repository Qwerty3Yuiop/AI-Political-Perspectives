Summary from the AllSides News Team

Several reports of American Jews being subjected to violence and harassment by pro-Palestinian groups arose this week amid the armed conflict between Israel and Hamas militants. In Los Angeles on Tuesday, a group of Jewish men was attacked while eating at a restaurant and suffered minor injuries. In New York City Thursday, Twitter video apparently showed pro-Palestinian people harassing diners at a restaurant. The Anti-Defamation League reported Thursday that "there have been numerous antisemitic incidents around the world related" to the Israel-Hamas violence. Israel and Hamas agreed to a ceasefire Thursday after fighting that left at least 12 Israelis and 230 Palestinians dead.

Reports of anti-Semitic attacks in the U.S. were highlighted prominently by right-rated outlets, which often included video of altercations and focused on different incidents around the country, including in New York, California, Florida and Illinois. Left- and center-rated sources generally covered the news less frequently; left-rated outlets that did cover the news typically focused on the incident in Los Angeles and local leaders' response to it.
