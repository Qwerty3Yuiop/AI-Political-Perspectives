Summary from the AllSides News Team

An investigation by the New York state Attorney General's office says that Gov. Andrew Cuomo (D) sexually harassed at least 11 women, including staffers and state employees, and fostered a “toxic culture” in his office. The probe began in March after several women accused Cuomo of sexual harassment. The report does not mention any specific penalties for Cuomo. The governor has previously denied allegations of misconduct; in virtual remarks released Tuesday, Cuomo said he "never touched anyone inappropriately" and pledged not to resign. New York lawmakers have reportedly already prepared articles of impeachment against Cuomo.

The news was the top story on most major news websites across the spectrum Tuesday afternoon. Many news sources, regardless of bias rating, highlighted the probe's finding that Cuomo also retaliated against at least one woman over her accusations; many also mentioned the separate probe Cuomo faces over his office's alleged cover-up of COVID-19-related deaths in nursing homes. Some coverage from right-rated sources focused on comments President Joe Biden made in March saying that Cuomo should resign if a probe confirmed the harassment allegations.
