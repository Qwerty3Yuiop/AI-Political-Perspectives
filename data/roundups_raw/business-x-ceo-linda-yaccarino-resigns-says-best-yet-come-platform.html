Summary from the AllSides News Team

X CEO Linda Yaccarino stepped down from her position on Wednesday, without offering a specific reason why.

Yaccarino’s Statement: Yaccarino wrote on X, “After two incredible years, I’ve decided to step down as CEO of X… I’m incredibly proud of the X team - the historic business turn around we have accomplished together has been nothing short of remarkable… Now, the best is yet to come as X enters a new chapter with xAI. X is truly a digital town square for all voices and the world’s most powerful culture signal. We couldn’t have achieved that without the support of our users, business partners, and the most innovative team in the world. I’ll be cheering you all on as you continue to change the world. As always, I’ll see you on X.” X owner Elon Musk replied, “Thank you for your contributions.”

For Context: Yaccarino, a former NBC executive, was hired as CEO in May 2023. X’s built-in artificial intelligence chatbot Grok came under fire earlier this week for antisemitic comments, shortly after an update to make it less “politically correct.” In one instance, Grok also made lewd comments about Yaccarino.

How The Media Covered It: Associated Press (Left bias) opened its article describing Yaccarino’s two years at X as “bumpy” and included a small portion of her quote before including several quotes from digital analysts. It also noted that she hasn’t commented on Grok’s antisemitic behaviors yet. Reuters (Center) framed the resignation as “a surprise move just months after the platform was acquired by the billionaire's AI startup, xAI.” Fox Business (Lean Right) led with a few positive snippets of Yaccarino’s statement.
