Summary from the AllSides News Team

Officials in the U.S. and Europe have sharply criticized Belarus for grounding a Ryanair commercial flight to arrest Roman Protasevich, a 26-year-old journalist who ran a messaging app used to coordinate protests against Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko. The plane was escorted to Minsk, Belarus by a fighter jet after it crossed over Belarusian airspace on its way to Vilnius, Lithuania from Athens, Greece. U.S. Secretary of State Anthony Blinken demanded the journalist’s release on Sunday, condemning “the Lukashenka regime’s brazen and shocking act.” The same day, Ryanair CEO Michael O’Leary called Belarus’ actions “a case of state-sponsored hijacking.” The European Commission will reportedly meet to discuss the situation on Monday. 

Coverage from across the spectrum was generally balanced, focusing on the widespread statements condemning Belarus’ actions.
