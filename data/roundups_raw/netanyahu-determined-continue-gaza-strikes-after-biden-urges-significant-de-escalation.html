Summary from the AllSides News Team

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said he was “determined to continue” Israeli operations in Gaza after President Joe Biden told him he “expected a significant de-escalation today on the path to a cease-fire” in a Wednesday phone call. This was the latest of four calls between the two leaders this week; Biden previously called Netanyahu and Palestinian Authority President Mahmoud Abbas on Saturday, reiterating his support for Israel’s right to self-defense and voicing concern over the bombing of journalists’ offices. As of Wednesday afternoon, the ongoing conflict between Hamas and Israel has led to 12 Israeli resident deaths and 227 Palestinian deaths; Gaza's dense population and lack of protections like Israel's Iron Dome defense system contributed to the disproportionate death counts. Coverage in right-rated outlets tended to frame Netanyahu as “brushing off” Biden and called Hamas a "terrorist organization." Coverage in left-rated outlets tended to focus more on deaths and damage from the conflict and called Hamas a "militant group."
