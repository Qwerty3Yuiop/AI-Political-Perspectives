Summary from the AllSides News Team

Early Wednesday morning, the U.S. House passed a bill to raise the debt ceiling by $2.5 trillion, hours after the Senate passed the same bill by a 50-49 vote. Every House Republican opposed the bill, except for Congressman Adam Kinzinger of Illinois.

The bill, which raises the debt limit to $31.4 trillion, avoids a federal default before the Dec. 15 deadline set by the Treasury Department. The bill is now headed to President Joe Biden's desk, and he is expected to sign it soon.
