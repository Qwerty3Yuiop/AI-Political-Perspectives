Summary from the AllSides News Team

A Washington Post report published Tuesday revealed 866 pages of leading disease expert Dr. Anthony Fauci's emails sent during the early months of the COVID-19 pandemic. The unveiled emails show Fauci's correspondence with business leaders, random strangers, colleagues and leaders from foreign governments, including George Gao, director of China's Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. At times, Fauci said he was sending up to 1,000 emails per day.

The Post described the emails as indicative of Fauci's struggle "to bring coherence to the Trump administration’s chaotic response to the virus." Reports from right-rated sources often framed the emails as evidence of Fauci being overly friendly toward Chinese health officials; many singled out a email from Fauci to Gao in which he said the two would "get through this together."
