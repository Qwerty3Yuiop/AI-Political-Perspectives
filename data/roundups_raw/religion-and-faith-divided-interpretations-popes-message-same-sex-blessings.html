Summary from the AllSides News Team

What will be the impact of Pope Francis’ letter approving the blessings of same sex couples under certain circumstances?

Deliberate Confusion: In The National Review (Right bias), Michael Brendan Dougherty (Lean Right bias) argued that the announcement contained a “deliberate” confusion regarding the Vatican’s stance on same sex unions that allows room for interpretation, writing that the document “must be accepted as orthodox, but another valid response is to reject it as insufficient and wish it had gone further.” Dougherty determines that the language of the text indicates same sex unions cannot be blessed, but individuals in same sex unions can be blessed individually. Overall, the language of the announcement sends the message that “as long as it all remains informal, priests can do what they want here.”

Incremental Change: A writer in CNN Opinion (Left bias) determined that the announcement will “bring hope” to LGBT Catholics seeking acceptance in the church, but does not revise Catholic doctrine. News coverage framing the document as an acceptance of same sex unions from the church is an example of the Pope “getting a lot of headlines for making cautious incremental change at the margins.” The writer determines that the vagueness of the document’s language diminishes its impact, framing it as another example of incremental change during Pope Francis’ decade at the head of the church that could “evaporate on his death, leaving behind some confusion, a lot of disappointment, and very few substantive reforms.”
