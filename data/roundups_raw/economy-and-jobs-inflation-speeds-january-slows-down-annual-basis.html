Summary from the AllSides News Team

The year-over-year inflation rate was 3.1% in January, down from 3.4% in December, the Bureau of Labor Statistics reported. 

The Details: Prices rose at a monthly rate of 0.3% in January, an acceleration from December’s 0.2% rate. Core inflation, a measure excluding volatile food and energy costs, rose 0.4% in January compared to 0.3% in December.  

How the Media Covered It: Depending on whether one looks at the monthly or annual number, the inflation data could be viewed as an increase or a decrease, allowing headlines to paint different pictures. While Fox Business (Lean Right bias) used a different photo with its article, it featured the story on its homepage with a photo of a burning wallet. Some left-rated sources such as CNN Business (Lean Left bias) focused their headlines on how the annual rate fell.
