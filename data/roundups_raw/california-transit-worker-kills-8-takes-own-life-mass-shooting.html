Summary from the AllSides News Team

A light rail transit employee opened fire at his workplace in San Jose, California on Wednesday morning, killing eight co-workers and wounding another before taking his own life. The gunman was identified as Samuel Cassidy, 57, according to the Santa Clara Sheriff’s Office. California Governor Gavin Newsom cancelled an event to rush to the scene. Newsom described gun violence in the U.S. as an epidemic, saying, “This happens again and again – rinse and repeat, rinse and repeat...It begs the question what the hell is going on in America?” President Joe Biden was briefed on the shooting Wednesday, and subsequently urged Congress to "take immediate action" on gun legislation. 

The story received wide coverage from across the political spectrum.
