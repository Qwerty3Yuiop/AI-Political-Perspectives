Summary from the AllSides News Team

Texas state Democratic lawmakers have left the state to prevent a vote on a proposed redistricting plan supported by President Donald Trump.

The Details: The redistricting plan, unveiled by Texas's majority Republicans last week, would create five new Republican-leaning seats in the US House of Representatives. 51 Democratic lawmakers have fled the state, most of them to Illinois, denying Republicans the required quorum to hold a vote. “This truancy ends now,” Texas Governor Greg Abbott said Sunday, warning that he will “remove the absent Democrats from their elected office” if they are not in attendance when the House reconvenes at 3:00 PM on Monday. State Democrats received the backing of national party figures, with House Minority Leader Hakeem Jeffries praising their "courage, conviction and character." Texas Republicans currently hold 25 out of 38 congressional seats in the state. The new maps could increase that number to 30.

For Context: This is the third time in recent years that Democrats have fled Texas to deny Republicans a quorum. The party's legislators left for Washington, DC in 2021 in an unsuccessful attempt to block the passage of new election rules. Texas Democrats also left for Oklahoma in 2003 in a bid to stop redistricting that Republicans eventually managed to get approved.

How The Media Covered It: The Guardian (Left bias) emphasized the Democrats' efforts to prevent the vote and highlighted the backing they received from national party figures. The Washington Examiner (Lean Right) reported on the potential implications of the redistricting for the 2026 midterm elections, noting Texas's increased significance in the upcoming political cycle. Breitbart (Right) spotlighted Texas Governor Greg Abbott's threat to remove from office any lawmakers who fail to return to Texas for a vote. BBC News (Center) detailed both the Democrats' reasons for leaving and the Republicans' response.
