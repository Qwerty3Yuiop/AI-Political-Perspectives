Summary from the AllSides News Team

The Wall Street Journal (Center bias) published a report citing multiple sources who've raised concerns about President Joe Biden's memory and performance in meetings.

The Details: Among the examples in the report was a January meeting about aid for Ukraine, in which Biden "read from notes to make obvious points, paused for extended periods and sometimes closed his eyes for so long that some in the room wondered whether he had tuned out," according to "five people familiar with the meeting." Former House Speaker Kevin McCarthy (R-CA) was among the few to criticize Biden on the record, saying, "I used to meet with him when he was vice president. I'd go to his house. He's not the same person."

Pushback: White House spokesperson Andrew Bates said it's "a little surprising that The Wall Street Journal thought it was breaking news when congressional Republicans told them the same false claims they've spouted on Fox News for years." Sen. Patty Murray (D-WA) said she told the Journal that Biden "was absolutely engaged & ran that [Ukraine] meeting in a way that brought everyone together."

How the Media Covered It: Some left-rated sources focused more on criticisms of the report from the White House and Democrats. NBC News (Lean Left) said the Journal "relies on Kevin McCarthy to criticize Biden." Right-rated sources focused more on the details of the Journal's report.
