Summary from the AllSides News Team

Dr. Vinay Prasad, Chief Officer of the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), was reinstated as director of the Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research on Saturday, after he resigned in late July amid political controversies. 

The Details: Prasad was reinstated following FDA Commissioner Marty Makary’s request, which reportedly prompted the White House to review the situation. MAGA activist Laura Loomer advocated for Prasad’s removal, accusing him of working against the Make America Healthy Again (MAHA) movement. She called the reinstation, “just more evidence of a broken vetting system with no standards.”

For Context: Prasad resigned after less than three months of federal employment, likely due to disputes with both Loomer and biopharmaceutical company Sarepta Therapeutics. 

Key Quote: Health and Human Services (HHS) spokesperson Andrew Nixon said on Saturday, “At the FDA’s request, Dr. Vinay Prasad is resuming leadership of the Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research. Neither the White House nor HHS will allow the fake news media to distract from the critical work the FDA is carrying out under the Trump administration.” 

How The Media Covered It: News media on the left covered Prasad’s departure and reinstation more broadly than media in the center and on the right, but outlets across the political spectrum noted that HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. and Makary opposed his resignation. Newsweek (Center bias) took a sensational approach, focusing on Loomer’s opposition and response. Both Politico (Lean Left) and Washington Examiner (Lean Right) highlighted the disputes among conservative officials.
