Summary from the AllSides News Team

President Joe Biden emailed his son Hunter's business partner dozens of times leading up to and after Hunter's appointment to the board of Burisma, a Ukrainian energy company, in 2014.

The Details: House Republicans released a document showing hundreds of emails sent between 2010 and 2019 from Joe Biden to Eric Schwerin, founding partner of Hunter’s now-defunct Rosemont Seneca Partners investment firm. The document doesn't include the messages sent but shows the email addresses used. Biden used multiple email aliases as part of the communications. IRS whistleblower Joseph Ziegler gave the committee the email data during a closed-door hearing earlier this month.

How the Right Covered It: Right-rated sources covered the document's release extensively when it happened. Many framed it as more evidence that Biden was improperly involved in his son's Ukrainian business dealings.

How Left and Center Covered It: AllSides only found two articles on the story from sources rated as being on the left or in the center. Utah-based Deseret News (Center bias) covered a local angle on the document on Dec. 6, and NBC News (Lean Left bias) covered it two weeks later on Dec. 21. Deseret linked to the document detailing the emails. NBC did not and said it had not reviewed it. NBC highlighted House Democrats who said Schwerin "worked as Joe Biden’s financial adviser from 2009 to 2017 and helped him file his tax returns." NBC's headline also focused on how House Republicans released the records, framing it as a partisan act.
