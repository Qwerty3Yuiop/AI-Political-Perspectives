Summary from the AllSides News Team

On Monday, Apple unveiled “Apple Intelligence,” the tech giant’s project to integrate artificial intelligence into Apple products.

Key Quote: Apple CEO Tim Cook stated during the presentation, “As we look to build in these incredible new capabilities, we want to be sure that the outcome reflects the principles at the core of our products. It has to be powerful enough to help the things that matter the most to you. It has to be intuitive and easy to do.” Apple executive Craig Federighi stated,  “Apple Intelligence is the personal intelligence system that puts powerful generative models right at the core of your iPhone, iPad and Mac. It draws on your personal context to give you intelligence that’s most helpful and relevant to you.”

How the Media Covered It: Coverage from the New York Times (Lean Left bias) stated that Apple “could do more than any other company to add credibility to a technology that has more than a few critics, who worry that it is mistake-prone and could add to the flood of misinformation already on the internet” and could “also help calm concerns that the iPhone maker had slipped behind its biggest rivals in the tech industry’s embrace of artificial intelligence.” The Wall Street Journal (Center bias) determined Apple previewed “what many consider to be the holy grail of AI, a voice assistant empowered with enough personal user information to meaningfully help complete an array of tasks.”
