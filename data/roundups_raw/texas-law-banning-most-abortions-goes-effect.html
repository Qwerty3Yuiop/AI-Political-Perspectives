Summary from the AllSides News Team

A Texas abortion law that bans abortions once a fetal heartbeat is detected took effect Wednesday after the Supreme Court decided not to act on an emergency petition filed by abortion providers to stop the measure. The Heartbeat Act (SB 8) bans abortions around the six-week mark, and allows individuals to sue anyone suspected of helping a woman get an abortion.

The law allows for a exemption for "medical emergencies," but does not provide an exception for rape or incest.  It is one of most restrictive abortion bans in the United States. Critics argue that many women don't yet know they're pregnant at the six-week mark; Texas state Sen. and sponsor of the bill Bryan Hughes (R) called it “the most powerful pro-life legislation in Texas history.”
