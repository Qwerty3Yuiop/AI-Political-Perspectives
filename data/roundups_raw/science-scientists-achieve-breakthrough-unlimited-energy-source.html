Summary from the AllSides News Team

Scientists at a U.S. government laboratory in California reportedly took a big step toward producing an unlimited, clean energy source, according to the Energy Department.

Key Quotes: The scientists "made a breakthrough in the pursuit of limitless, zero-carbon power by achieving a net energy gain in a fusion reaction for the first time," as first reported in an anonymously-sourced story from the Financial Times (Center bias) that was then confirmed by multiple news outlets.

In Layman's Terms: Scientists have long been working on developing nuclear fusion, the energy source that powers the sun. Successful nuclear fusion reactions require only a small amount of hydrogen to conduct and generate large amounts of energy without emitting carbon or lasting radioactive waste.

Where People Agree: Many sources across the spectrum highlighted the development as major and objectively positive. Many headlines emphasized that the scientists are based in the U.S., and how other scientists around the world have also been approaching the breakthrough.

Where People Disagree: Some framed fusion energy as a potential additional option alongside common energy sources. Others framed it as an outright replacement and solution in the context of pollution and climate change. CNN described it as a step to unleashing "an infinite source of clean energy that could help end dependence on fossil fuels." Some were outwardly critical. Bloomberg (Lean Left bias)'s energy columnist said on Twitter that he's "skeptical of surprisingly well-timed announcements by budget-starved laboratories about breakthroughs for technologies decades away." Updated 12/13/22 at 1:07 p.m. ET with the Energy Department's announcement.
