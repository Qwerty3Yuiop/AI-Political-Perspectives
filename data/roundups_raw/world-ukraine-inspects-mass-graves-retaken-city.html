Summary from the AllSides News Team

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Thursday that a "mass burial site" was discovered in the recently liberated city of Izyum in eastern Ukraine.

Ukrainian officials told multiple news sources that at least 400 bodies were thought to be buried at the site, which is marked by hundreds of wooden crosses in a forest near the city. Officials will begin exhuming the graves on Friday to look for signs of torture and war crimes. Russia took control of Izyum early on in the invasion, and used it as a key military hub for its effort to conquer the Donbas region in eastern Ukraine. The recent Ukrainian counteroffensive pushed Russians out of the city.

A senior adviser to Zelenskyy said that "many places of mass burials" and "many places where people were tortured" had been found in other retaken cities. When Ukraine retook the city of Bucha in April, hundreds of bodies of civilians were discovered in mass graves and in the streets, sparking accusations of Russian war crimes.

The mass graves in Izyum were covered prominently by news sources across the spectrum, and sources on all sides highlighted similar aspects of the story.
