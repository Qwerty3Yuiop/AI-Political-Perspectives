Summary from the AllSides News Team

Germany has announced that it is halting certification of the Nord Stream 2 pipeline amid the threat of a Russian invasion of Ukraine.

The 764-mile pipeline would carry natural gas to Germany from Russia and would be operated by a subsidiary of Russia state company Gazprom. German Chancellor Olaf Scholz said that the pipeline "cannot go online" while Russian troops are in eastern Ukraine. While Germany considers other ways to secure its energy needs, the U.S. continues to assert that Europe would become too dependent on Russia for its natural gas if the Nord Stream 2 certification process is completed.

The Russia-Ukraine crisis continues to make top headlines across the spectrum.
