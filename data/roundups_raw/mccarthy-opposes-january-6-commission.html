Summary from the AllSides News Team

On Tuesday, House Republican Leader Kevin McCarthy said he opposes a proposal to form an independent, bipartisan commission to investigate the violence at the U.S. Capitol on January 6. McCarthy argues that the commission would fail to investigate last summer's protests and riots across American cities, and accuses Democrats of over-politicizing the situation. McCarthy's opposition likely erodes GOP support ahead of a vote later this week. His opposition follows accusations from Representative Liz Cheney – who was ousted from her GOP leadership role with McCarthy’s support – that he withheld critical information about his response to the events of Jan. 6.
