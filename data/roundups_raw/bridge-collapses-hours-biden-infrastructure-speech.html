Summary from the AllSides News Team

A snow-covered bridge in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania collapsed on Friday just hours before President Joe Biden was scheduled to visit the city to speak about the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.

At least 10 people sustained non-life threatening injuries in the bridge collapse. “We saw today, when a bridge is in disrepair, it literally can threaten lives,” Biden said, adding, “We’re gonna rebuild that bridge, along with thousands of other bridges in Pennsylvania and across the country.” The $1.2 trillion infrastructure bill, which the president signed into law in November, invests $40 billion over five years to repair bridges that are in “fair or poor” condition.

Some coverage from the right focused on a specific Biden quote: “I knew there were a lot of bridges … And we’re gonna fix them all.” A Fox News (Right) homepage headline said Biden made a “huge promise.” Some coverage from the left drew a direct connection between the collapse and Biden’s infrastructure bill; a Guardian (Lean Left) article opened with the line, “It would be hard to imagine a more dramatic way to illustrate the need for investment in U.S. infrastructure.”

President Joe Biden made an unscheduled visit to the site of a bridge collapse in Pittsburgh on Friday afternoon, during a trip originally meant to tout his legislative agenda — including the landmark infrastructure law he signed last year.
