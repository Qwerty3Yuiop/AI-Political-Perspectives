Summary from the AllSides News Team

Police in Ottawa, Canada began arresting 'Freedom Convoy' protesters Thursday after weeks of disruptive demonstrations against COVID-19 public health restrictions.

One of the multiple people arrested is one of the leaders of the demonstration; he faces a class-action lawsuit from a local resident who complained that the protests were disrupting the community. Officials had already begun cracking down on protesters after Prime Minister Justin Trudeau invoked the country's Emergencies Act, and reports across the spectrum highlighted how financial accounts of some protesters and funders of the convoy were frozen. Police also arrested 11 people after discovering a cache of weapons, including body armor and high-capacity firearm magazines, among protesters at a US-Canada border crossing in Alberta.

Reports from left- and center-rated outlets often focuses on alleged crimes of the protesters and frames them as dangerous or disruptive. Coverage from right-rated sources tends to frame the Canadian government's reaction to the protests as oppressive. One AllSides team member compared defenses of the Freedom Convoy from the right to defenses of George Floyd protests from the left.

Canadian authorities have arrested 11 people after discovering an arsenal of weapons among protesters at the U.S. border in Alberta. The Royal Canadian Mounted Police were tipped off about an armed subset embedded in the widespread protests, and proceeded to find guns, ammunition, a machete, body armor, and high-capacity firearm magazines in three trailers, per CBC.
