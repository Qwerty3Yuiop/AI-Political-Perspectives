Summary from the AllSides News Team

Florida Gov. Ron DeSantis (R) signed into law a bill prohibiting transgender women from competing in female sports at public schools and colleges on Tuesday. The Fairness in Women's Sports Act requires transgender athletes to compete on the team of the biological sex displayed on their birth certificate. "We believe that is very important that the integrity of these competitions are preserved," DeSantis said. "We're gonna go based on biology, not based on ideology when we're doing sports." Selina Soule, a Connecticut high school track athlete who was bested by transgender athlete Terry Miller in 2019, spoke at the bill signing, saying the experience was “frustrating” and “demoralizing.” The Human Rights Campaign said Tuesday it plans to challenge the legislation in court. 

Many on the right praised DeSantis for "protecting" women's sports. Many Democrats claim the bill is transphobic and discriminatory, and that signing the bill during Pride Month is "additionally cruel"; DeSantis said the timing was coincidental. Most outlets covered the details of the bill; some right-rated outlets covered the backlash DeSantis received from those on the left.

Florida has become the latest US state to ban transgender girls and women from participating in female sports at public schools and colleges.

The state's Republican governor Ron DeSantis signed the measure into law on Tuesday.

The law says women and girls must play on the teams of the biological sex on their birth certificate.

LGBT activists have denounced the move as "discriminatory", with one group vowing to launch a legal challenge.
