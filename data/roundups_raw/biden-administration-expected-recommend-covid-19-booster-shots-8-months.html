Summary from the AllSides News Team

The Biden administration is expected to recommend COVID-19 booster shots after eight months for most Americans.  The recommendation could come as early as this week, and the boosters could be administered sometime in September, pending FDA approval.

Federal heath officials have been considering this recommendation after reportedly looking at preliminary studies from other countries that suggest the vaccine's protection against serious illness drops off after eight months. The World Health Organization and other global health authorities have on more-vaccinated and wealthier countries to hold off on booster shots until third-world countries have received the first dose of the vaccine; the U.S. has shared more than 100 million vaccine doses.
