Summary from the AllSides News Team

On Saturday, Texas Republicans unveiled the final version of The Election Integrity Protection Act of 2021, after it was revised by a conference committee led by Sen. Bryan Hughes and Rep. Briscoe Cain. The new version of the bill would prohibit election officials from mailing all voters absentee ballots, and would ban drive-thru and 24-hour voting. It would also add new requirements for mail-in voting, and would prohibit the use of tents, garages and mobile units as polling places. The bill has strong support from GOP lawmakers, who have a majority in both branches of Texas' legislature. Gov. Greg Abbott (R) has also said he will sign the measure, which Democrats said they would challenge in court. President Joe Biden called the Texas bill  “wrong and un-American" in a statement Saturday. The bill will be sent to both chambers for a simple majority vote this weekend.

Coverage from left- and center-rated outlets tended to focus on the details of the bill and provided analysis on its likelihood of passing, as well as backlash from Biden and other Democratic lawmakers. As of Saturday afternoon, the story received little to no coverage from right-rated outlets besides Fox News.
