Summary from the AllSides News Team

A Harvard professor has been convicted of concealing ties to a Chinese-run program that seeks to recruit American scientists to help advance China's scientific development.

Harvard University chemistry professor Charles Lieber was found guilty this week of making false statements to authorities, filing false tax returns and failing to report a Chinese bank account. Lieber was convicted by a jury in Massachusetts federal court; the jury concluded that he lied to government investigators about his participation in the Chinese government’s Thousand Talents program, which seeks to recruit foreign experts to work for China, and that he failed to report cash payments from the program. Prosecutors showed evidence that Lieber signed an agreement through the program with the Wuhan University of Technology that paid him hundreds of thousands of dollars, some of which he received via deposits into a Chinese bank account.

The guilty verdict was covered prominently by sources across the spectrum Wednesday.
