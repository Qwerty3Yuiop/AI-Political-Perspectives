Summary from the AllSides News Team

On Thursday, the Labor Department reported that initial unemployment claims rose to 206,000 last week.  The number was higher than expected but still remained near the lowest level in decades.

Employers remain hesitant to lay off workers in the face of a tight labor supply.  The labor force participation rate was 61.8% in November, compared to 63.3% in February, and the size of the civilian labor force is down by 2.4 million.

New applications for unemployment benefits edged higher last week but stayed very low, reflecting an unusually tight labor market as the economy continued to recover.

Initial jobless claims, a proxy for layoffs, inched up by 18,000 to 206,000 for the week ended Dec. 11 from a revised 188,000—the lowest level in 52 years—the week before, the Labor Department said Thursday. New jobless claims have been steadily declining throughout the year.
