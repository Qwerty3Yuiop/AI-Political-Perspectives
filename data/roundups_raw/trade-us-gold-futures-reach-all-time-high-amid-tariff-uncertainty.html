Summary from the AllSides News Team

In the face of uncertainty over whether US import tariffs would be applied to commonly traded sizes of gold bars, US gold futures have hit a record high. 

The Details: The Trump administration clarified that import tariffs would apply to one-kilo gold bars, according to the Wall Street Journal (Center bias). But Bloomberg (Lean Left bias) suggested that the administration will implement a policy excluding gold bars from tariffs, saying, "The White House intends to post an executive order in the near future to clarify what an official called misinformation about the tariffing of gold and other specialty products," based on an anonymous source. 

The Numbers: The potential imposition of country-specific import tariffs on the most widely traded gold bullion bars in the US was revealed in a ruling on the US Customs and Border Protection service’s website on Friday. In response to this news, December US gold futures rose 1.2%, peaking at a record $3,534.10 per ounce, according to Reuters (Center). The spread between US gold futures and spot prices has widened, currently sitting at $91. 

For Context: This development comes after Switzerland, the world's leading hub for gold refining and transit, failed to negotiate lower levies on its exports to the US, which currently stand at 39%. Amid this uncertainty, some gold refineries, including a large Swiss entity, have paused deliveries of bullion to the US.
