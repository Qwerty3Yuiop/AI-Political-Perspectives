Summary from the AllSides News Team

President Joe Biden formally declared Pride Month on Tuesday, saying, "we recognize the resilience and determination of the many individuals who are fighting to live freely and authentically." This marked a departure from former President Donald Trump, who never acknowledged the celebration outside of a 2019 tweet thread. Pride Month received its first official declaration from former President Bill Clinton in June 1999. Also on Tuesday, Florida Gov. Ron DeSantis signed a law effectively banning transgender student athletes from participating on teams associated with their gender identity, the latest in a series of Republican-backed state bills regarding transgender rights.

Left- and center-rated outlets were more likely to publish opinion articles about Pride Month, with some outlets on all sides covering news stories like Biden's statement. Perspectives varied by topic, with some focusing on the celebration itself and others discussing recent controversies.
