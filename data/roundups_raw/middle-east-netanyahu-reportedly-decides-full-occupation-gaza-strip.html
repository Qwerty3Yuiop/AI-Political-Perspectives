Summary from the AllSides News Team

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu reportedly decided to fully occupy Gaza, sparking controversy within Israel and abroad due to the ongoing war with Hamas and the humanitarian crisis in the region.

The Details: According to multiple sources, Netanyahu's decision has been communicated to Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir, the army's Chief of Staff, with an ultimatum to either agree with the decision or resign. Expansion of the occupation is expected to include operations in areas where hostages are being held.

For Context: Israel has been at war with Hamas for 21 months, a conflict that has resulted in tens of thousands of deaths and a severe humanitarian crisis in Gaza. Hamas continues to hold 50 Israeli hostages, with only 20 believed to be alive. There has been growing international pressure for a ceasefire, but negotiations have been fraught with difficulties. The largest known evacuation of wounded children from Gaza to the US is also expected to be carried out by the non-profit organization HEAL Palestine this week.

How the Media Covered It: Mediaite (Lean Left bias) emphasized the criticism Netanyahu faces over his handling of the war, and highlighted his refusal to engage in ceasefire talks despite calls from the international community. On the other hand, the New York Post (Lean Right) focused on the belief of senior Israeli officials that Hamas will not free the remaining hostages without being forced to surrender. The publication also noted that there is opposition within Israel to the decision, warning that full occupation could lead to a "security, humanitarian and diplomatic disaster." Newsweek (Center) reported on both the decision and the criticism Netanyahu faces, but also acknowledged the impact of recent videos showing emaciated hostages on the Israeli public.
