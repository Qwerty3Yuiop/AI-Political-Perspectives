Summary from the AllSides News Team

President Joe Biden waived executive privilege on the first set of documents requested by the House committee investigating the Jan. 6 Capitol riot, White House Press Secretary Jen Psaki announced Friday. In doing so, Biden rejected a request by former President Donald Trump to withhold the documents under executive privilege, setting up a potential legal battle. In a letter to the National Archives, which currently possesses the documents, White House Counsel Dana Remus argued that executive privilege "should not be used to shield, from Congress or the public, information that reflects a clear and apparent effort to subvert the Constitution itself.” Also on Friday, it was reported that former Trump adviser Steve Bannon would defy the House committee’s subpoena, while former Trump aides Mark Meadows and Kash Patel were engaging with the committee. 

Coverage was more prominently featured in left- and center-rated outlets on Friday. Coverage differed on describing Biden’s move as a standalone decision or as a rejection of Trump’s request to withhold the documents.
