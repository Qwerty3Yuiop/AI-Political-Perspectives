Summary from the AllSides News Team

Since late 2023, the IRS has recovered $1.3 billion in unpaid taxes from high-income Americans, according to the Treasury Department.

The Details: The money was collected due to new initiatives launched by the IRS following increased funding from the Inflation Reduction Act passed in 2022. In February 2024, the IRS began pursuing 125,000 “high-income, high-wealth taxpayers” who had not filed taxes since 2017. Six months later, around 21,000 of those taxpayers had filed their taxes, amounting to $172 million in recovered funds.

For Context: In a 2019 speech, Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen said the top one percent of wealthy Americans owed more than one-fifth of all unpaid taxes.

How the Media Covered It: Media across the spectrum focused on the improvements made to the IRS. The Washington Times (Lean Right bias) also included Republican concerns that President Joe Biden "supercharged" the IRS.
