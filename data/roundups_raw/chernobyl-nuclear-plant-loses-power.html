Summary from the AllSides News Team

According to the State Nuclear Regulatory Inspectorate of Ukraine, the Chernobyl nuclear site has lost power.

Emergency diesel generators will provide power to critical safety systems at the site for up to 48 hours, but at present, repair to the transmission lines is not possible due to continued combat operations in the area. There are approximately 20,000 spent fuel assemblies at Chernobyl that require constant cooling, which is provided by electricity.  The fear is that the temperature in the holding pools will increase, prompting the release of radioactive substances into the environment. The International Atomic Energy Association, however, says there is no critical impact on safety, and that the amount of cooling water at Chernobyl is "sufficient for effective heat removal without need for electrical supply."

Russian forces seized the Chernobyl site on February 24, and captured Zaporizhzhia, Europe's biggest nuclear plant, on March 4.
