Summary from the AllSides News Team

The Israeli Security Cabinet approved a plan to expand military action in Gaza Friday morning, which includes taking control of Gaza City. 

The Details: Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has stated that the goal of this operation is to ensure Israel's security, remove Hamas from power, and allow for the return of hostages. The plan to occupy Gaza City, devised by Netanyahu, goes against recommendations from his military's chief of staff. Netanyahu's office announced five steps for concluding the war, including returning the hostages, disarming Hamas, demilitarizing the Gaza Strip, taking security control of the territory, and establishing "an alternative civil administration that is neither Hamas nor the Palestinian Authority."  Netanyahu said Israel does not want to govern Gaza: “We want to hand it over to Arab forces.” 

For Context: The Israeli military currently controls about 75% of Gaza, and this new operation could lead to the occupation of the entire Gaza Strip, but the plan Netanyahu announced stopped short of saying they would occupy the entire strip. "Trump said this week that the question of taking over all of Gaza was 'pretty much up to Israel,' indicating that he would not interfere in Jerusalem’s decision-making on the matter," According to the Times of Israel (Center bias). Hamas said Israel is choosing to "sacrifice" hostages over its Gaza City takeover and Germany has halted arms exports to Israel over the plan.

How The Media Covered It: The New York Times (Lean Left bias) focused on the potential humanitarian consequences of the operation, noted the plan went against military recommendations, and included perspectives from civilians in Gaza. BBC News (Center) detailed the criticism of Israel from other nations, while The Times of Israel included more detail about US involvement in the plan. The Washington Examiner (Lean Right) highlighted Netanyahu's goal to root out Hamas and pressure the group to release remaining Israeli hostages and mentioned US President Donald Trump's lack of opposition to the plan in its short article.
