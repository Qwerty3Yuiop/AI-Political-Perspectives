Summary from the AllSides News Team

In 2005, the United Nations designated January 27 as International Holocaust Remembrance Day. This was the day in 1945 that the Auschwitz-Birkenau concentration camp was liberated by the Soviet army.

A small ceremony will take place at the site of the former Auschwitz death camp in Poland, where at least 1.1 million people were killed. Many other Holocaust Remembrance Day events will be held virtually around the world. 

Outlets across the spectrum recognized the day of remembrance and highlighted the commemorative events taking place around the world. Coverage on all sides also highlighted reports of rising antisemitism and Holocaust misinformation on social media.
