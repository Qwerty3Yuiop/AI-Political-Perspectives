Summary from the AllSides News Team

On Wednesday, former President Donald Trump filed a petition asking the Supreme Court to keep his name on the ballot in Colorado.

Key Details: The petition for writ of certiorari appeals to the Supreme Court to reverse the Colorado supreme court and add Trump's name to the ballot in Colorado for this year's presidential election. In their decision, the Colorado supreme court cited a constitutional provision barring anyone from holding public office who engaged in rebellion or insurrection. Trump's filing increases the likelihood that the Supreme Court will take this case and provide a uniform decision nationwide soon.

Key Quote: Trump's lawyers wrote: “The issues presented in this petition are of exceptional importance and urgently require this court’s prompt resolution".

For Context: Colorado was the first state, followed by Maine, to rule that Trump should be removed from the state's ballot due to his actions ahead of the January 6, 2021, attack on the U.S. Capitol.

How the Media Covered it: Sources across the political spectrum noted the increasing need for a nationwide resolution to the question of Trump's eligibility. Sources on the right noted that the Supreme Court might be reluctant to hear the case for fear of being pulled into a political firestorm.
